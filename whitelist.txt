E-mail and Internet companies use blockers to stop spam. Sometimes these programs block e-mail you want to get. We ask that you add us to your trusted list of senders, contacts or address book. All also known as "Whitelisting."

If you do not see an email from us in your Inbox, our email may have mistakenly been sent to your spam folder.

Please <strong>open your spam folder</strong> and if you find an email from us, open it and mark it as <strong>"Not spam"</strong>...

Of course, every e-mail system is different. Below are more specific instructions for some of the more popular ones.

<strong>Is your email client or spam filter not listed?</strong>

One thing you can do no matter what e-mail system you’re using is add the address in the “From” line of your most recent subscription email to your address book.

If messages continue to be sent to your junk folder contact your ISP or spam filter application support and ask how to whitelist the email. If you’re using some sort of spam filtering or blocking software yourself (in addition to what your ISP provides), we’ve also listed instructions on how to exempt your subscription from some of the more popular of those programs.

Sending Address: If they ask for our address, give them the address in the “From” line of your most recent subscription email.

Domain: If they need to know the domain we’re mailing from, tell them:
xxxxx.com

<h3 id="aol">AOL</h3>
<h6><strong>WebMail</strong></h6>
<ul>
    <li>Open the email.</li>
    <li>Click on the sender's name and email address.</li>
    <li>Click "Add to Address Book" in the window that appears.</li>
    <li>Enter any additional information.</li>
    <li>Click Save.</li>
</ul>
<h6><strong>AOL (version 9.0 or higher)</strong></h6>
<ul>
    <li>Copy the "from" address from the latest email (or you can copy the addresses listed above).</li>
    <li>In the pop-up box, select Add.</li>
    <li>Paste the copied address/type the address required in the Other email field.</li>
    <li>Make the newly added address the "Primary email address" by checking the checkbox.</li>
    <li>Select Save.</li>
</ul>
<h6><strong>AOL 8.0</strong></h6>
<ul>
    <li>Select Mail in the Menu on the top of the screen.</li>
    <li>Select Mail Controls.</li>
    <li>The Mail Control Screen would be displayed. Select Next.</li>
    <li>Select Customize Mail Controls for this Screen Name and click Next.</li>
    <li>You can select "Allow email from all AOL members, email addresses and domains" (allows all emails).</li>
    <li>Select Next until the Save button shows up at the bottom.</li>
    <li>Select Save.</li>
</ul>
<h6><strong>AOL 7.0</strong></h6>
<ul>
    <li>Select Mail in the Menu on the top of the screen.</li>
    <li>Select Mail Controls.</li>
    <li>The Mail Control Screen would be displayed. Select Next.</li>
    <li>Select Customize Mail Controls for this Screen Name and click Next.</li>
    <li>In the exclusion and inclusion parameters, include the domain xxxxx.com.</li>
</ul>

    <li>Select the Add Filter button once again.</li>
</ol>

<h3 id="road">Road Runner</h3>

<ol>

    <li>Open your Junk Mail folder.</li>
    <li>Select the email that includes the e-mail address you would like to add as a safe sender.</li>
    <li>Click "Mark as Not Spam".</li>
</ol>
<h3 id="spam">Spam Assassin</h3>
<ol>
    <li>Find the spam assassin folder on your hard drive, and open it.</li>
    <li>There would be a file named user_prefs. Open it with a text editor (such as Notepad).</li>
    <li>If there is no such file, create it, instructions are available at this <a href="https://spamassassin.taint.org/doc/Mail_SpamAssassin_Conf.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener">website</a>.</li>
    <li>Make a new line in the file. On that line, enter whitelist_from, followed by the address that you wish to whitelist. To whitelist the domain, enter @xxxxx.com.</li>
    <li>Save and close the file.</li>
</ol>
